Audit Summary

Website: embryo-ai.co.za

Audit Date: 2025-09-21

Prepared by: Jimmy Motsei, MaruOnline

Scope: Public-facing, non-intrusive triage of the live site

Executive Summary

The new site is live and reachable at https://www.embryo-ai.co.za/. The homepage appears to return an empty HTML document to non‑JavaScript clients (likely a JS‑rendered SPA or bot-blocking). This can impact discoverability (basic crawlers/social link previews), and may delay first content on slower mobile networks—common across South Africa.

Priority Fixes (next 3–5 days):

  • Ensure server‑rendered HTML (SSR) or prerender for core routes (Home, Services, Contact).
  • Verify robots.txt and sitemap.xml are present and correct.
  • Ship essential meta in initial HTML (title, meta description, canonical, Open Graph/Twitter).
  • Add/verify security headers (HSTS, CSP, X‑Content‑Type‑Options, X‑Frame‑Options, Referrer‑Policy).
  • Smoke‑test contact flows (forms, tel:, mailto:, WhatsApp).

What We Checked (public-only)

  • Homepage fetch, basic HTML presence, and rendering risk indicators.
  • High‑level SEO surface: robots/sitemap conventions, meta expectations, structured data posture (not validated yet).
  • Performance posture assumptions for African mobile networks (LCP/INP risks with JS‑only render).
  • Accessibility surface (headings/landmarks/meta expectations).
  • Security headers posture (assumed defaults; cannot confirm without headers/logs).

Key Findings

Rendering / Discoverability

  • Homepage HTML returns empty to non‑JS fetchers, indicating client‑side render without SSR/prerender.
  • Impact: Some crawlers and social bots won't see content/metadata; previews may be blank; performance may suffer due to late paint/hydration.

SEO Surface

  • robots.txt / sitemap.xml: Could not be confirmed publicly in this pass.
  • Impact: Missing or incorrect files hinder discovery and timely indexing, especially for a new launch.
  • Meta in initial HTML: Not verifiable from a non‑rendered response.
  • Impact: Titles/descriptions/OG cards may not appear on shares; ranking snippets may be sub‑optimal.

Performance (Core Web Vitals posture)

  • Risk of slow LCP/INP on mobile due to JS‑only render, large bundles, and late hydration (typical for SPA defaults).
  • Africa context: Variable bandwidth and higher RTTs amplify these costs; prioritize critical CSS/HTML and progressive rendering.

Accessibility

  • Without SSR, semantics and landmarks may not be available to basic agents pre‑render. Ensure proper headings order, aria‑labels, focus states, and keyboard operability in the shipped HTML.

Security & Trust

  • HTTP security headers (HSTS, CSP, etc.) not observable in this pass; small sites frequently omit these by default.
  • Platform note (Lovable): As with any hosted builder, apply strict content policies and link hygiene. Ensure any third‑party scripts and form endpoints are trusted and version‑pinned.
